1. OVERVIEW
================================================================================

The Accounts module is the financial backbone of uOrgHub ERP. It handles all
money-related operations for your civil construction business, including:

  - Double-entry bookkeeping (Journal Entries)
  - Accounts Receivable (Customers, Invoices, Receipts)
  - Accounts Payable (Vendors, Bills, Payments)
  - Bank account management and reconciliation
  - Budget planning and variance tracking
  - Tax configuration (VAT, WHT, etc.)
  - Chart of Accounts and cost center structure

All transactions in other modules (Procurement, Payroll, Projects) that involve
money eventually flow into the Accounts module as journal entries.

Required Permission : Accounts.View  (to view all pages)
                      Accounts.Create (to create records)
                      Accounts.Edit   (to edit records)
                      Accounts.Delete (to delete records)
                      Accounts.PostJournal (to post journal entries)

Your system administrator assigns these permissions per role.

================================================================================
2. ACCESSING THE ACCOUNTS MODULE
================================================================================

1. Log in to uOrgHub with your username and password.
2. In the left sidebar, click "Accounts".
3. The Accounts Dashboard opens, showing a summary of your financial position.
4. Use the sidebar sub-menu or the dashboard quick-links to navigate to any
   sub-section (Chart of Accounts, Invoices, etc.).

If the Accounts menu does not appear, contact your administrator — you may not
have the Accounts.View permission assigned.

3. INITIAL SETUP (RECOMMENDED ORDER)
================================================================================

Before recording any financial transactions, complete setup in this order:

  Step 1 — Account Groups        (classify account types)
  Step 2 — Fiscal Years          (define your financial year)
  Step 3 — Chart of Accounts     (create your GL account list)
  Step 4 — Cost Centers          (define departments/project codes)
  Step 5 — Tax Rates             (set up VAT, WHT, etc.)
  Step 6 — Bank Accounts         (link physical bank accounts)
  Step 7 — Customers             (add your clients)
  Step 8 — Vendors               (add your suppliers)

Once setup is complete, you can begin recording Invoices, Bills, Payments,
Journal Entries, and Budgets.

4. ACCOUNT GROUPS
================================================================================

Path: Accounts > Account Groups

PURPOSE
-------
Account Groups classify all GL accounts into five standard accounting types.
Every account in your Chart of Accounts must belong to one group.

The five standard types are:
  - Asset      (what the company owns: cash, receivables, equipment)
  - Liability  (what the company owes: payables, loans)
  - Equity     (owner's funds and retained earnings)
  - Income     (revenue earned from projects, services)
  - Expense    (costs incurred: materials, salaries, utilities)

CREATING AN ACCOUNT GROUP
--------------------------
1. Click "Add Group" in the top-right corner.
2. Fill in the form:
     Group Name  : A clear, descriptive name (e.g., "Current Assets")
     Code        : A short unique code (e.g., "CA")
     Type        : Select from Asset / Liability / Equity / Income / Expense
     Description : Optional explanation
3. Click "Save".

EDITING AN ACCOUNT GROUP
-------------------------
1. Click the pencil (edit) icon next to the group.
2. Modify the fields as needed.
3. You can also toggle the "Active" checkbox to deactivate a group.
4. Click "Save".

DELETING AN ACCOUNT GROUP
--------------------------
1. Click the trash icon next to the group.
2. Confirm deletion.
   NOTE: You cannot delete a group that has accounts assigned to it.
   Deactivate it instead.

SEARCHING
----------
Use the search bar above the table to filter groups by name, code, or type.

RECOMMENDED GROUPS FOR CIVIL CONSTRUCTION
------------------------------------------
  Code   | Name                       | Type
  -------|----------------------------|----------
  CA     | Current Assets             | Asset
  FA     | Fixed Assets               | Asset
  CL     | Current Liabilities        | Liability
  LTL    | Long-term Liabilities      | Liability
  EQ     | Equity                     | Equity
  REV    | Project Revenue            | Income
  COGS   | Cost of Goods Sold         | Expense
  OPEX   | Operating Expenses         | Expense

5. FISCAL YEARS
================================================================================

Path: Accounts > Fiscal Years

PURPOSE
-------
A Fiscal Year defines the financial reporting period. All invoices, bills,
journal entries, and budgets must be linked to a fiscal year. You can have
multiple fiscal years (past, current, future).

CREATING A FISCAL YEAR
-----------------------
1. Click "Add Fiscal Year".
2. Fill in the form:
     Name       : e.g., "FY 2025-2026"
     Start Date : First day of the financial year (e.g., 01 July 2025)
     End Date   : Last day of the financial year (e.g., 30 June 2026)
     Status     : Set to "Pending" when creating future years
3. Check "Set as current fiscal year" if this is your active year.
4. Click "Save".

MANAGING STATUS
----------------
  Pending  → Active  : When the year begins (set as current)
  Active   → Closed  : When the year ends (year-end closing)

  Only one fiscal year can be "Active" and "Current" at a time.
  Transactions cannot be recorded in a Closed fiscal year.

EDITING A FISCAL YEAR
----------------------
1. Click the edit icon.
2. You can change the name, dates, status, or current year flag.
3. You cannot delete a fiscal year that has transactions linked to it.

6. CHART OF ACCOUNTS
================================================================================

Path: Accounts > Chart of Accounts

PURPOSE
-------
The Chart of Accounts (COA) is the complete list of all general ledger (GL)
accounts your company uses to record financial transactions. Every debit and
credit in the system posts to a GL account.

CREATING AN ACCOUNT
--------------------
1. Click "Add Account".
2. Fill in the form:
     Account Code     : Unique code (e.g., "1001" for Cash)
     Account Name     : Descriptive name (e.g., "Cash in Hand")
     Account Group    : Select from your defined groups
     Account Type     : Asset / Liability / Equity / Income / Expense
     Opening Balance  : Starting balance (for existing companies migrating data)
     Description      : Optional notes
     Allow Direct Entry: Check this to allow manual journal entries to this
                         account. Uncheck for system-controlled accounts
                         (e.g., AR control account).
3. Click "Save".

EDITING AN ACCOUNT
-------------------
1. Click the pencil icon next to the account.
2. Edit name, group, description, or toggle Active status.
   NOTE: Account Code cannot be changed after creation.
3. Click "Save".

DELETING AN ACCOUNT
--------------------
You cannot delete an account that has posted transactions.
Deactivate it using the Active toggle instead.

ACCOUNT NUMBERING CONVENTION (RECOMMENDED)
--------------------------------------------
  1xxx  — Assets
  2xxx  — Liabilities
  3xxx  — Equity
  4xxx  — Revenue / Income
  5xxx  — Cost of Goods Sold
  6xxx  — Operating Expenses
  7xxx  — Other Income/Expense

7. COST CENTERS
================================================================================

Path: Accounts > Cost Centers

PURPOSE
-------
Cost Centers let you track income and expenses by department, project, or
location. In construction, this is typically used to track costs per project

CREATING A COST CENTER
-----------------------
1. Click "Add Cost Center".
2. Fill in the form:
     Code        : Short unique code (e.g., "SITE-A", "HQ", "PRJ-001")
     Name        : Full descriptive name
     Parent      : Optional — select a parent for a hierarchical structure
                   (e.g., "Site A - Block 1" under "Site A")
     Description : Optional notes
3. Click "Save".

EDITING A COST CENTER
----------------------
1. Click the pencil icon.
2. You can update name, parent, description, or toggle Active.
   NOTE: Code cannot be changed after creation.

HIERARCHY EXAMPLE
------------------
  HEAD OFFICE (HQ)
    ├── Finance Department
    ├── HR Department
  SITE OPERATIONS
    ├── Bashundhara Residential (PRJ-001)
    ├── Gulshan Commercial (PRJ-002)
    └── Mirpur Road Bridge (PRJ-003)

When recording invoices, bills, or journal entries, you can tag a cost center
to track which project or department the cost/income belongs to.

8. TAX RATES
================================================================================

Path: Accounts > Tax Rates

PURPOSE
-------
Tax Rates define the tax percentages applied to invoice lines and bill lines.
Common taxes in Bangladesh include VAT (15%) and Withholding Tax (WHT).

CREATING A TAX RATE
--------------------
1. Click "Add Tax Rate".
2. Fill in the form:
     Code        : Short code (e.g., "VAT15", "WHT5")
     Name        : Full name (e.g., "VAT @ 15%", "WHT on Services @ 5%")
     Tax Type    : Select from:
                     VAT            — Value Added Tax
                     WithholdingTax — Deducted at source by buyer
                     CustomsDuty    — Import duties
                     ExciseDuty     — Excise / manufacturing tax
                     SalesTax       — General sales tax
     Rate (%)    : Numeric percentage (e.g., 15.00 for 15%)
     Description : Optional notes
3. Click "Save".

EDITING A TAX RATE
-------------------
1. Click the pencil icon.
2. Update the name, rate, description, or Active status.
   NOTE: Tax Code cannot be changed after creation.

IMPORTANT NOTES
---------------
- Tax amounts are calculated automatically when you select a tax rate on an
  invoice line or bill line.
- A corresponding Tax Payable / Tax Receivable GL account should exist in your
  Chart of Accounts.
- Deactivated tax rates will not appear in invoice/bill dropdowns.

COMMON TAX RATES FOR BANGLADESH
---------------------------------
  Code   | Name                            | Type           | Rate
  -------|---------------------------------|----------------|------
  VAT15  | VAT @ 15%                       | VAT            | 15.00
  WHT5   | WHT on Services @ 5%            | WithholdingTax |  5.00
  WHT3   | WHT on Contractors @ 3%         | WithholdingTax |  3.00
  AIT    | Advance Income Tax @ 1%         | WithholdingTax |  1.00

9. BANK ACCOUNTS & TRANSACTIONS
================================================================================

Path: Accounts > Bank Accounts

PURPOSE
-------
Bank Accounts registers all company bank accounts and allows you to record
individual transactions (deposits, withdrawals, transfers, cheque clearing).

---------------------------------------
9A. MANAGING BANK ACCOUNTS
---------------------------------------

ADDING A BANK ACCOUNT
----------------------
1. Click "Add Bank Account".
2. Fill in the form:
     Account Number   : Your bank account number
     Account Name     : Description (e.g., "HSBC Current Account")
     Bank Name        : e.g., "Dutch-Bangla Bank", "BRAC Bank"
     Branch Name      : Branch where account is held (optional)
     Routing Number   : Bank routing/SWIFT code (optional)
     Currency         : BDT / USD / EUR / GBP
     Opening Balance  : Starting balance when migrating to this system
     Linked GL Account: Select the corresponding GL account from your
                        Chart of Accounts (e.g., account "1002 - Cash at Bank")
3. Click "Save".

EDITING A BANK ACCOUNT
-----------------------
1. Click the pencil icon next to the account.
2. Update name, bank, branch, or active status.
   NOTE: Account Number cannot be changed after creation.

---------------------------------------
9B. RECORDING BANK TRANSACTIONS
---------------------------------------

1. In the bank accounts list, click "Transactions" (or the view icon) next to
   any bank account to open its transaction history.
2. Click "Add Transaction".
3. Fill in the form:
     Transaction Type : Select from:
                          Deposit       — Money received into account
                          Withdrawal    — Money paid out of account
                          Transfer      — Move funds between accounts
                          Fee           — Bank charges/fees
                          Interest      — Interest earned
                          ChequeDeposit — A cheque received and deposited
                          ChequeIssue   — A cheque issued/written
     Date             : Transaction date
     Amount           : Transaction amount
     Reference Number : Bank reference / transaction ID (optional)
     Description      : What this transaction is for (required)
     Cheque Number    : For cheque transactions (optional)
     Payee            : Recipient or payer name (optional)
4. Click "Save".

TRANSACTION TABLE
-----------------
Each transaction row shows:
  Date | Type | Description | Reference | Payee | Amount | Reconciled

Deposits appear as positive (green) amounts.
Withdrawals appear as negative (red) amounts.
Reconciled transactions are marked "Yes".

NOTE: Bank transactions should ideally be matched to Payments (Section 14) for
proper double-entry accounting. Direct bank entries are for adjustments,
charges, and opening balance entries.

11. INVOICES (ACCOUNTS RECEIVABLE)
================================================================================

Path: Accounts > Invoices

PURPOSE
-------
Invoices record amounts your clients owe you for completed work, services, or
project milestones. When posted, they create journal entries that debit
Accounts Receivable and credit Revenue accounts.

INVOICE LIFECYCLE
------------------
  Draft → Sent → PartiallyPaid → Paid
              ↘ Overdue
              ↘ Void / Cancelled

CREATING AN INVOICE
--------------------
1. Click "New Invoice".
2. Fill in the invoice header:
     Invoice Number  : Auto-generated or enter manually (e.g., "INV-2025-001")
     Customer        : Select from your customer list
     Fiscal Year     : Select the current fiscal year
     Invoice Date    : Date the invoice is issued
     Due Date        : Payment due date (Invoice Date + Payment Terms)
     Cost Center     : Optional — link to a project or department
     Notes           : Any special instructions or terms

3. Add line items in the table:
   For each line click "Add Line" and fill in:
     Description     : What was delivered or completed
     Quantity        : Number of units
     Unit Price      : Price per unit
     Discount %      : Any line discount (0 if none)
     Tax Rate        : Select applicable tax (e.g., VAT 15%) or leave blank
     Revenue Account : Select the revenue GL account (e.g., "4001 Project Revenue")

   The system automatically calculates:
     Line Total = (Quantity × Unit Price) − Discount + Tax

4. Review the subtotal at the bottom.
5. Click "Save" to save as Draft.

POSTING AN INVOICE
-------------------
A Draft invoice is not yet in your books. To record it in the general ledger:
1. Find the invoice in the list (status: Draft).
2. Click the "Send" button (checkmark/send icon).
3. The invoice status changes to "Sent".
4. A journal entry is automatically created:
     DR  Accounts Receivable   [invoice total]
     CR  Revenue Account(s)    [line amounts]
     CR  VAT Payable           [tax amounts]

VOIDING AN INVOICE
-------------------
1. Find the invoice (status: Draft or Sent).
2. Click the void icon (X button).
3. Confirm the action.
4. Status changes to "Void". If already posted, a reversing journal entry
   is created automatically.
   NOTE: Paid or PartiallyPaid invoices cannot be voided.

VIEWING INVOICE LINES
----------------------
Click the expand arrow on any invoice row to see all line item details:
Description | Qty | Unit Price | Disc% | Tax Amount | Line Total

SEARCHING & FILTERING
----------------------
- Use the search bar to find invoices by invoice number or customer name.
- Use the Status filter dropdown to show only: Draft / Sent / PartiallyPaid /
  Paid / Overdue / Cancelled / Void invoices.

13. BILLS (ACCOUNTS PAYABLE)
================================================================================

Path: Accounts > Bills

PURPOSE
-------
Bills record amounts your company owes to vendors for materials, services,
or subcontract work. When approved, they create journal entries that debit
Expense accounts and credit Accounts Payable.

BILL LIFECYCLE
--------------
  Draft → Received → PartiallyPaid → Paid
                  ↘ Overdue
                  ↘ Void / Cancelled

CREATING A BILL
----------------
1. Click "New Bill".
2. Fill in the bill header:
     Bill Number       : Your internal bill number (e.g., "BILL-2025-001")
     Vendor Bill Number: The supplier's own invoice number (optional)
     Vendor            : Select from your vendor list
     Fiscal Year       : Select the current fiscal year
     Bill Date         : Date you received the vendor's invoice
     Due Date          : Payment due date
     Cost Center       : Optional — link to a project or department
     Notes             : Any notes or special instructions

3. Add line items in the table:
   For each line click "Add Line" and fill in:
     Description     : What was purchased or delivered
     Quantity        : Number of units
     Unit Price      : Price per unit
     Discount %      : Any line discount (0 if none)
     Tax Rate        : Select applicable tax (e.g., VAT 15%) or leave blank
     Expense Account : Select the expense GL account
                       (e.g., "5001 - Material Cost", "6001 - Salaries")

   The system automatically calculates:
     Line Total = (Quantity × Unit Price) − Discount + Tax

4. Click "Save" to save as Draft.

APPROVING A BILL
-----------------
A Draft bill is not yet in your books. To record it:
1. Find the bill in the list (status: Draft).
2. Click the approve button (checkmark icon).
3. The bill status changes to "Received".
4. A journal entry is automatically created:
     DR  Expense Account(s)    [line amounts]
     DR  VAT Receivable/Input  [tax amounts]
     CR  Accounts Payable      [bill total]

VOIDING A BILL
---------------
1. Find the bill (status: Draft or Received).
2. Click the void icon (X button).
3. Confirm the action.
4. Status changes to "Void". If already posted, a reversing journal entry
   is created automatically.
   NOTE: Paid or PartiallyPaid bills cannot be voided.

VIEWING BILL LINES
-------------------
Click the expand arrow on any bill row to see all line item details.

SEARCHING & FILTERING
----------------------
- Use the search bar to find bills by bill number or vendor name.
- Use the Status filter to show specific bill statuses.

14. PAYMENTS
================================================================================

Path: Accounts > Payments

PURPOSE
-------
Payments record actual money movements — receiving payment from a customer or
paying a vendor. Payments can be allocated to specific invoices or bills to
clear the outstanding balance.

PAYMENT TYPES
--------------
  CustomerPayment      — Cash/bank received from a customer
  VendorPayment        — Cash/bank paid to a vendor
  AdvanceToVendor      — Advance payment before receiving a bill
  AdvanceFromCustomer  — Advance received before issuing an invoice
  Refund               — Money returned to a customer or from a vendor

RECORDING A PAYMENT
--------------------
1. Click "Record Payment".
2. Fill in the payment details:
     Payment Number  : Auto-generated or enter manually (e.g., "PAY-2025-001")
     Payment Date    : Date the payment was made or received
     Payment Type    : Select the type (see above)
     Payment Method  : Select how payment was made:
                         Cash | BankTransfer | Cheque | CreditCard |
                         DebitCard | MobileBanking | OnlineTransfer
     Customer        : Required for CustomerPayment / AdvanceFromCustomer
     Vendor          : Required for VendorPayment / AdvanceToVendor
     Amount          : Total payment amount
     Bank Account    : Select which bank account was used (optional)
     Reference No.   : Bank reference, transfer ID, etc. (optional)
     Cheque Number   : For cheque payments (optional)
     Fiscal Year     : Select the active fiscal year
     Notes           : Any additional notes

3. ALLOCATING TO INVOICES / BILLS (optional but recommended):
   After selecting a Customer or Vendor, a list of open invoices/bills
   appears at the bottom.

   For each invoice/bill you want to settle:
   a. Click "Allocate" next to the invoice/bill.
   b. Enter the amount to allocate (up to the invoice/bill balance).
   c. The "Unallocated Amount" at the top reduces as you allocate.

   A payment can be split across multiple invoices/bills.
   Any unallocated amount remains as advance/credit on the account.

4. Click "Save".

WHAT HAPPENS WHEN YOU SAVE
----------------------------
A journal entry is automatically created. For a customer payment:
     DR  Bank / Cash Account   [payment amount]
     CR  Accounts Receivable   [allocated amount]
     CR  Advance from Customer [unallocated amount, if any]

For a vendor payment:
     DR  Accounts Payable      [allocated amount]
     DR  Advance to Vendor     [unallocated amount, if any]
     CR  Bank / Cash Account   [payment amount]

VIEWING ALLOCATIONS
--------------------
Click the expand arrow on any payment row to see the invoice/bill allocations:
Invoice/Bill Number | Allocated Amount

IMPACT ON INVOICE/BILL STATUS
-------------------------------
When a payment is fully allocated:
  - Invoice moves from Sent → PartiallyPaid (if partial) or Paid (if full)
  - Bill moves from Received → PartiallyPaid (if partial) or Paid (if full)

15. JOURNAL ENTRIES
================================================================================

Path: Accounts > Journal Entries

PURPOSE
-------
Journal Entries are the foundation of double-entry accounting. Every financial
transaction — whether from an invoice, bill, payment, or manual entry — is
recorded as a journal entry. Each entry must balance: Total Debits = Total Credits.

Most journal entries are created automatically when you post invoices, approve
bills, or record payments. Manual journal entries are used for:
  - Depreciation entries
  - Accruals and provisions
  - Opening balance entries
  - Corrections and adjustments
  - Month-end and year-end adjustments

JOURNAL ENTRY LIFECYCLE
------------------------
  Draft → Posted
        ↘ Cancelled

CREATING A MANUAL JOURNAL ENTRY
---------------------------------
1. Click "New Entry".
2. Fill in the header:
     Entry Date       : Date the transaction occurred
     Reference Number : Optional (e.g., "DEPR-MAY-2026")
     Description      : Clear description of the transaction (required)

3. Add journal lines (minimum 2 lines required):
   Click "Add Line" for each line and fill in:
     Account     : Select the GL account from Chart of Accounts
     Description : Line-level note (optional)
     Debit       : Enter a debit amount (leave 0 for credit lines)
     Credit      : Enter a credit amount (leave 0 for debit lines)

   IMPORTANT: Enter amounts in either Debit OR Credit — never both on the
   same line. Each line is either fully debit or fully credit.

4. The system shows running totals:
     Total Debits  | Total Credits | Balance

   The "Submit" button is disabled until Debits = Credits.

5. Click "Save" to save as Draft.

POSTING A JOURNAL ENTRY
------------------------
Posting finalises the entry and updates all GL account balances.
1. Find the entry (status: Draft).
2. Click the "Post" button (send icon).
3. The entry status changes to "Posted".
   NOTE: Posted entries cannot be edited. To correct a posted entry,
   you must cancel it (which creates a reversal) and post a new corrected entry.

CANCELLING A JOURNAL ENTRY
----------------------------
1. Find the Draft entry.
2. Click the Cancel button (X icon).
3. Status changes to "Cancelled".
   NOTE: Posted entries require administrator intervention to cancel.

VIEWING ENTRY LINES
--------------------
Click the expand arrow on any journal entry row to see all lines:
Account Code & Name | Description | Debit | Credit

EXAMPLE: MONTHLY DEPRECIATION ENTRY
--------------------------------------
  Entry Date   : 31 May 2026
  Description  : Monthly depreciation — excavator & equipment
  Reference    : DEPR-MAY-2026

  Lines:
    Account                        | Debit    | Credit
    -------------------------------|----------|--------
    Depreciation Expense (6010)    | 50,000   |
    Accumulated Depreciation (1510)|          | 50,000

16. BUDGETS
================================================================================

Path: Accounts > Budgets

PURPOSE
-------
Budgets define planned spending and income for a fiscal year. Once activated,
actual figures from journal entries are compared to the budget, giving you
variance analysis (over/under budget).

BUDGET LIFECYCLE
-----------------
  Draft → Approved → Active → Closed
        ↘ Cancelled

CREATING A BUDGET
------------------
1. Click "New Budget".
2. Fill in the budget header:
     Budget Name  : e.g., "FY 2025-26 Operating Budget"
     Fiscal Year  : Select the fiscal year this budget covers
     Cost Center  : Optional — create a budget for a specific department
                    or project
     Description  : Optional notes

3. Add budget lines:
   Click "Add Line" for each account you want to budget, then fill in:
     Account       : Select the GL account from Chart of Accounts
     Period        : Choose:
                       Annual   — One budget amount for the whole year
                       Month 1  — January (or your year's first month)
                       Month 2  — February
                       ...
                       Month 12 — December (or your year's last month)
     Planned Amount: The budgeted amount for this account and period

   You can add multiple lines for the same account across different periods
   (monthly budgeting).

4. The "Total Planned" at the bottom shows the sum of all budget lines.
5. Click "Save" to save as Draft.

APPROVING A BUDGET
-------------------
1. Find the budget (status: Draft).
2. Click the Approve button (checkmark icon).
3. Status changes to "Approved" → then "Active" when the fiscal year starts.

EDITING A BUDGET
-----------------
Only Draft budgets can be edited. Click the pencil icon to open.

DELETING A BUDGET
------------------
Only Draft budgets can be deleted. Active or Approved budgets cannot be deleted
— cancel them instead.

VIEWING VARIANCE ANALYSIS
--------------------------
Click the expand arrow on any budget row to see all budget lines with:
  Account | Period | Planned Amount | Actual Amount | Variance

Variance = Actual − Planned
  Green (positive)  — Actual is less than planned (under budget for expenses,
                       or more than planned for revenue) = favorable
  Red (negative)    — Actual exceeds planned (over budget for expenses) = adverse

18. WORKFLOW SUMMARY
================================================================================

ACCOUNTS RECEIVABLE WORKFLOW (Getting paid by clients)
--------------------------------------------------------
  1. Add Customer  →  2. Create Invoice  →  3. Post Invoice
  →  4. Record Payment (CustomerPayment, allocate to invoice)
  →  Invoice status: Paid ✓

ACCOUNTS PAYABLE WORKFLOW (Paying suppliers)
---------------------------------------------
  1. Add Vendor  →  2. Create Bill  →  3. Approve Bill
  →  4. Record Payment (VendorPayment, allocate to bill)
  →  Bill status: Paid ✓

BANK RECONCILIATION WORKFLOW
------------------------------
  1. Record bank transactions (deposits, withdrawals, fees)
  2. Match transactions to payments already recorded
  3. Mark reconciled transactions
  4. Bank balance should match GL bank account balance

MONTH-END CLOSING WORKFLOW
----------------------------
  1. Ensure all invoices for the month are posted
  2. Ensure all bills for the month are approved
  3. Ensure all payments are recorded and allocated
  4. Record manual journal entries (depreciation, accruals, prepayments)
  5. Verify Trial Balance: Total Debits = Total Credits
  6. Run budget variance review

YEAR-END CLOSING WORKFLOW
--------------------------
  1. Complete all month-end tasks for the final month
  2. Post closing journal entries (transfer P&L to retained earnings)
  3. Set current fiscal year status to "Closed"
  4. Create and activate the new fiscal year
  5. Enter opening balances for the new year (if needed)

19. STATUS REFERENCE
================================================================================

INVOICE STATUSES
-----------------
  Draft          — Created, not yet posted to GL. Can be edited or deleted.
  Sent           — Posted to GL. AR debited, Revenue credited.
  PartiallyPaid  — One or more payments received but balance still outstanding.
  Paid           — Fully settled. No balance due.
  Overdue        — Due date passed, balance still outstanding.
  Cancelled      — Voided before posting. No GL impact.
  Void           — Reversed after posting. Reversing entry created in GL.

BILL STATUSES
--------------
  Draft          — Created, not yet posted to GL. Can be edited or deleted.
  Received       — Posted to GL. Expense debited, AP credited.
  PartiallyPaid  — One or more payments made but balance still outstanding.
  Paid           — Fully settled.
  Overdue        — Due date passed, balance still outstanding.
  Cancelled      — Voided before posting.
  Void           — Reversed after posting.

JOURNAL ENTRY STATUSES
-----------------------
  Draft          — Saved but not posted. Can be edited or cancelled.
  Posted         — Finalised and recorded in GL. Cannot be edited.
  Cancelled      — Reversed or discarded.

BUDGET STATUSES
----------------
  Draft          — Being prepared. Can be edited or deleted.
  Approved       — Authorised but year not yet started.
  Active         — Current budget period. Variance tracking is live.
  Closed         — Fiscal year ended. Historical reference only.
  Cancelled      — Abandoned. No financial impact.

FISCAL YEAR STATUSES
---------------------
  Pending        — Future year. Not yet active.
  Active         — Current financial year. Transactions can be recorded.
  Closed         — Past year. No new transactions allowed.

20. FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS
================================================================================

Q: Can I edit a posted journal entry?
A: No. Once a journal entry is posted, it cannot be edited. To correct it,
   cancel the entry (which creates a reversal) and post a new correct entry.
   Cancellation of posted entries may require administrator permission.

Q: Can I delete a customer or vendor with transactions?
A: No. Customers and vendors with existing invoices, bills, or payments cannot
   be deleted. Deactivate them using the Active toggle — they will be hidden
   from dropdowns but their history is preserved.

Q: What happens if I void a paid invoice?
A: Paid or PartiallyPaid invoices cannot be voided. You must first reverse the
   payment allocations before voiding the invoice. Contact your administrator.

Q: How do I handle a customer advance (payment before invoice)?
A: Record a payment with type "AdvanceFromCustomer". No invoice is needed.
   When you later raise the invoice, record a CustomerPayment and allocate it
   to the invoice. The advance balance will reduce.

Q: Can I have multiple active fiscal years?
A: No. Only one fiscal year can be "Current" at a time. However, you can have
   a Pending (future) year and a Closed (past) year existing simultaneously.

Q: Why is the Submit button greyed out in Journal Entries?
A: The debit and credit totals are not equal. Double-entry accounting requires
   that every entry is balanced. Review your line amounts until the balance
   shows zero.

Q: How do I record a bank charge or interest?
A: Go to Bank Accounts > select the account > Add Transaction.
   Use type "Fee" for bank charges and "Interest" for interest earned.
   For proper double-entry, also create a matching journal entry:
     Bank Charge: DR Bank Charges Expense | CR Cash at Bank
     Interest:    DR Cash at Bank          | CR Interest Income

Q: Can I budget by month?
A: Yes. When adding budget lines, select "Month 1" through "Month 12" for the
   Period field to set monthly targets per account. You can mix annual and
   monthly lines in the same budget.

Q: How do I track project-specific costs?
A: Assign a Cost Center to your invoices, bills, and journal entries. Create
   one Cost Center per project. Variance reports will then show actual vs.
   planned figures per project.

Q: What permissions do I need?
A: Your administrator assigns permissions. The minimum for read-only access
   is Accounts.View. To create and edit, you need Accounts.Create and
   Accounts.Edit. To post journal entries, you need Accounts.PostJournal.
